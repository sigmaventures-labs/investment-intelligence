# Thematic Screening — Real Estate

## Asset Map Format

```
### Tier 1 — Direct Beneficiaries
| Property Type | Market(s) | Mechanism | Conviction | Cap Rate Impact | NOI Exposure |

### Tier 2 — Indirect Beneficiaries (Second-Order)
| Property Type | Market(s) | Mechanism | Conviction | Cap Rate Impact | NOI Exposure |

### Tier 3 — Non-Obvious / Adjacent
| Property Type | Market(s) | Mechanism | Conviction | Cap Rate Impact | NOI Exposure |

### Negative Exposure — Impaired / Dislocated
| Property Type | Market(s) | Mechanism | Conviction | Cap Rate Impact | NOI Exposure |
```

For publicly traded REITs, add ticker column and use the equity format alongside.

## Key Exposure Metrics

- **NOI exposure**: What percentage of a portfolio or asset's NOI is affected
  by the theme? A mixed-use property with 20% retail exposure to food service
  is less affected than a single-tenant restaurant NNN lease.
- **Cap rate impact**: Directional estimate of cap rate compression or expansion
  from the theme (expressed in bps). This is the primary value lever.
- **Replacement cost relevance**: Does the theme make new construction more or
  less economic? Affects supply pipeline.

## "Priced In" Assessment

- Check transaction cap rates vs. 12-month trailing — has pricing already moved?
- Review REIT sector performance for public market read-through
- Assess capital flows: are allocators already rotating toward/away from the
  affected property types?
- Check construction pipeline: has supply already responded to the demand signal?
- Survey broker sentiment and leasing velocity trends

## Investability Filters

- **Vehicle**: Direct acquisition, REIT (public), private fund, debt (CMBS)?
  Each has different access, liquidity, and minimum commitment
- **Market depth**: Sufficient deal flow in the affected markets/property types?
- **Basis achievability**: Can you acquire at a basis that reflects your thesis,
  or has pricing already adjusted?
- **Financing availability**: Is debt available for the property type at
  acceptable terms? Some themes (e.g., office distress) face financing headwinds.

## RE-Specific Causal Chain Nodes

When decomposing a thesis, think through these real estate-specific transmission
mechanisms:

- **Demand drivers**: Population/job growth, remote work patterns, demographic
  shifts, industry clustering, immigration, institutional demand
- **Supply dynamics**: Construction pipeline, entitlement timelines, building
  material costs, zoning changes, conversion feasibility
- **Capital markets**: Cap rate movements, debt availability/pricing, CMBS
  spreads, foreign capital flows, REIT discount/premium to NAV
- **Regulatory**: Rent control, zoning reform, tax incentives (Opportunity Zones,
  LIHTC, 1031 exchange rules), environmental requirements
- **Operational**: Property management technology, insurance costs, property
  tax reassessment, utility/energy costs

## Example Causal Chain — "Remote Work Permanently Reduces Office Demand"

1. **First-order**: CBD Class A office (vacancy ↑, NOI ↓, cap rates widen)
2. **Second-order losers**: Retail/F&B dependent on office foot traffic,
   parking garages, office-adjacent hotels
3. **Second-order beneficiaries**: Suburban flex/coworking, residential in
   former office-dominant CBDs (conversion candidates), data centers (cloud
   infrastructure for remote work)
4. **Third-order**: Suburban industrial last-mile (residential demand shift),
   sunbelt multifamily (migration from HCOL), suburban retail (spending follows
   population), municipal bond risk (CBD tax base erosion)
