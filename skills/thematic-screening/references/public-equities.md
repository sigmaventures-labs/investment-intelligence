# Thematic Screening — Public Equities

## Asset Map Format

```
### Tier 1 — Direct Beneficiaries
| Company | Ticker | Mechanism | Conviction | Mkt Cap | Rev Exposure % |

### Tier 2 — Indirect Beneficiaries (Second-Order)
| Company | Ticker | Mechanism | Conviction | Mkt Cap | Rev Exposure % |

### Tier 3 — Third-Order / Non-Obvious
| Company | Ticker | Mechanism | Conviction | Mkt Cap | Rev Exposure % |

### Short Candidates — Losers / Dislocated
| Company | Ticker | Mechanism | Conviction | Mkt Cap | Rev Exposure % |
```

## Key Exposure Metric

**Revenue exposure %** — Estimate what percentage of revenue is thematically
exposed. A company with 5% exposure is less interesting than one with 40%.
Cross-reference with segment disclosures in 10-K filings where available.

## "Priced In" Assessment

- Check YTD and 12-month stock performance for theme-correlated moves
- Review sell-side coverage: how many analysts mention the theme in recent notes?
- Check short interest and options positioning for crowdedness signals
- Compare current valuation multiples to historical range — premium may already
  reflect the theme

## Investability Filters

- **Liquidity**: Average daily volume × price > minimum threshold for fund mandate
  (typically $5M+ ADV for SM funds, $20M+ for larger)
- **Market cap floor**: Per fund mandate
- **Index membership**: Relevant for benchmark-aware strategies
- **ADR/access**: For international names, can you trade them efficiently?

## Example Causal Chain — "GLP-1 Adoption Reshapes Consumer Staples"

1. **First-order**: GLP-1 manufacturers (NVO, LLY)
2. **Second-order losers**: Snack companies (MDLZ, HSY), bariatric surgery
   (GMED), diabetes devices (DXCM, PODD)
3. **Second-order beneficiaries**: Protein brands, activewear (LULU, NKE)
4. **Third-order**: Life insurance (lower mortality), airlines (fuel savings
   from lower avg passenger weight), food packaging (smaller portions)
