# Thematic Screening — Private Equity

## Asset Map Format

```
### Tier 1 — Direct Beneficiaries
| Sector/Sub-sector | Deal Archetype | Mechanism | Conviction | Typical Entry Multiple | Value Creation Lever |

### Tier 2 — Indirect Beneficiaries (Second-Order)
| Sector/Sub-sector | Deal Archetype | Mechanism | Conviction | Typical Entry Multiple | Value Creation Lever |

### Tier 3 — Non-Obvious / Adjacent
| Sector/Sub-sector | Deal Archetype | Mechanism | Conviction | Typical Entry Multiple | Value Creation Lever |

### Negative Exposure — Impaired / At Risk
| Sector/Sub-sector | Deal Archetype | Mechanism | Conviction | Exit Risk | Portfolio Impact |
```

## Key Exposure Metrics

- **Value creation lever**: Which PE value creation mechanism does the theme
  unlock? Revenue acceleration, margin expansion, multiple expansion,
  platform roll-up, or operational transformation?
- **Entry multiple sensitivity**: How does the theme affect what you should
  pay? Themes that are already consensus drive entry multiples up and compress
  returns.
- **Hold period relevance**: Is this a 3-year catalyst-driven theme or a
  10-year secular shift? Affects fund structure and underwriting.

## "Priced In" Assessment

- Check recent transaction multiples in the affected sectors — have they
  already expanded?
- Review GP fundraising: are new thematic funds launching in the space?
  (indicates capital chasing the theme)
- Assess deal flow competitiveness: more bidders = priced in
- Check public market comps: if public multiples have already re-rated,
  private will follow with a lag
- Survey placement agent sentiment and LP appetite for the theme

## Investability Filters

- **Fund vs. direct**: Can you express the theme through existing fund
  commitments, co-investment, or does it require a new GP relationship?
- **Deal flow access**: Are there enough actionable deals in the theme?
  Niche themes may have limited deal flow.
- **Operating capability**: Does the theme require specialized operating
  expertise (e.g., healthcare regulatory, real assets management)?
- **Hold period alignment**: Does the theme's catalyst timeline match
  fund life?
- **Dry powder deployment**: Can the theme absorb meaningful capital or
  is it a small-check niche?

## PE-Specific Causal Chain Nodes

When decomposing a thesis, think through PE-specific transmission mechanisms:

- **Roll-up / consolidation**: Fragmented industries affected by the theme
  that are ripe for platform acquisitions
- **Carve-out / divestiture**: Large corporates that will divest non-core
  assets affected by the theme
- **Operational transformation**: Businesses where the theme creates an
  opportunity to apply PE operational playbooks (pricing, procurement,
  technology, management upgrade)
- **Regulatory arbitrage**: Themes that create regulatory complexity favoring
  well-capitalized PE-backed operators
- **Capital structure**: Themes affecting leverage availability, refinancing
  risk, or optimal capital structure
- **Exit pathway**: How does the theme affect exit multiples and exit route
  (strategic sale, IPO, secondary, continuation)?
- **GP-LP dynamics**: Does the theme affect fund structures, LP appetite,
  or GP fundraising?

## Example Causal Chain — "AI Transforms Professional Services"

1. **First-order**: AI-native services platforms (consulting, legal tech,
   accounting automation) — PE targets for growth equity
2. **Second-order beneficiaries**: IT services roll-ups (bolt-on AI capability
   to platform), data infrastructure companies, AI training/staffing
3. **Second-order losers**: Traditional staffing firms (volume decline),
   mid-tier consulting (margin pressure), offshore BPO (automation replaces
   labor arbitrage)
4. **Third-order**: PE firms with large professional services portfolios
   face margin disruption (portfolio risk), but also transformation
   opportunity; new fund strategies around "AI-enabling" portfolio companies;
   LP pressure on GPs to demonstrate AI integration across portfolios
