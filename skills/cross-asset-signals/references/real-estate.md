# Cross-Asset Signals — Real Estate

## RE-Specific Signal Layer

In addition to the core five lenses, real estate investors should monitor:

### Real Estate Capital Markets
- **CMBS spreads**: AAA and BBB tranches — cost of securitized RE debt and
  risk appetite. Widening BBB = stress in lower-quality RE credit.
- **REIT implied cap rates**: Public REIT pricing implies a cap rate you can
  compare to private market transactions. Divergence = arbitrage signal.
- **REIT discount/premium to NAV**: Persistent discounts suggest private
  market values are too high; premiums suggest private is undervalued.
- **CRE CLO issuance**: Volume signals appetite for transitional/value-add
  lending. Slowdown = tighter bridge/construction financing.
- **CMBS delinquency rates**: Leading indicator of stress by property type.

### Real Estate Fundamentals
- **Construction starts**: Forward supply signal by property type and market.
  Spike in starts = future vacancy risk.
- **Leasing velocity / absorption**: Demand signal. Slowing absorption with
  rising starts = supply/demand imbalance brewing.
- **Cap rate transaction data**: Broker surveys (CBRE, JLL, Green Street) —
  are private market cap rates compressing or widening?
- **Rent growth indices**: CoStar, Zillow, Apartments.com — momentum and
  deceleration signals by property type.

### Insurance & Operating Cost Signals
- **Property insurance market**: Hardening insurance market is a material
  cost headwind, especially coastal/disaster-prone markets.
- **Property tax assessment trends**: Lagged but significant NOI impact.
- **Construction cost indices**: Affect replacement cost (valuation floor)
  and renovation/development economics.

## RE-Specific Playbooks

- **Rates falling + CMBS spreads tightening + REIT premium to NAV**
  → Private market cap rate compression coming; bid aggressively
- **Rates rising + CMBS widening + REIT discount to NAV**
  → Private market repricing needed; patience, distressed opportunities coming
- **CMBS delinquencies rising in office + IG credit stable**
  → Sector-specific stress, not systemic; surgical approach
- **Construction starts collapsing + leasing velocity stable + rates peaking**
  → Supply shortfall developing; current assets appreciate, new development
  will be very well-positioned
- **Insurance costs spiking + property taxes rising + rent growth decelerating**
  → NOI margin compression; re-underwrite existing portfolio, favor NNN leases
