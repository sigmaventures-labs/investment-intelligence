# Cross-Asset Signals — Private Equity

## PE-Specific Signal Layer

In addition to the core five lenses, PE investors should monitor:

### Leveraged Finance Markets
- **Leveraged loan prices** (S&P/LSTA index): The primary PE financing market.
  Falling prices = tighter financing, wider spreads, lower leverage available.
- **HY new issuance volume**: Signals the financing window. Heavy issuance =
  strong market; drought = deals harder to finance.
- **Leveraged loan spread to base rate**: Direct cost of LBO financing.
  +50bps spread → ~0.5x turn of leverage lost → material IRR impact.
- **CLO formation**: Primary demand driver for leveraged loans. Slowdown in
  CLO formation → reduced loan demand → wider spreads.
- **Direct lending spreads**: Private credit market pricing, increasingly
  relevant for middle-market PE.

### M&A & Deal Activity
- **M&A volume and multiples**: Are deal multiples expanding or compressing?
  Tracks closely to PE entry prices.
- **PE deal volume**: Are other GPs deploying or sitting on dry powder?
  Low volume = difficult environment but potential bargains.
- **Take-private activity**: High take-private volume = GPs see public
  market undervaluation. Low = public market fairly priced.
- **Sponsor-to-sponsor secondary volume**: Increasing volume can signal
  GPs struggling to find strategic exits.

### LP Market & Fundraising
- **PE fundraising volume**: Are LPs committing fresh capital? Slowdown =
  denominator effect, potential liquidity pressure.
- **Secondary market pricing**: Discount to NAV on LP interests signals
  LP liquidity stress and potential forced selling.
- **Distribution/contribution ratio**: Are GPs returning capital faster than
  calling it? Low D/C ratio = LP liquidity strain.
- **Dry powder levels**: Aggregate uninvested capital. High dry powder =
  competitive bidding on deals.

### Portfolio Health Signals
- **Public market comparable multiples**: Lead private market valuations
  with a lag. Contracting public multiples = future NAV write-downs.
- **Small/mid-cap earnings trends**: PE portfolio companies are typically
  small/mid-cap. Their earnings trajectory matters more than mega-cap.
- **Wage inflation / labor availability**: Key operating cost for PE portcos,
  especially services and healthcare.

## PE-Specific Playbooks

- **Leveraged loan prices falling + HY spreads widening + CLO issuance stalling**
  → Financing window closing; pause new deals, pre-emptively extend portfolio
  company maturities
- **Rates falling + credit tightening easing + public multiples recovering**
  → Exit window opening; accelerate sell-side processes
- **Secondary pricing at 85-90 cents + fundraising slow + D/C ratio low**
  → LP stress; potential to buy quality assets at discount via GP-led secondaries
- **PE dry powder high + M&A multiples elevated + leverage >6x available**
  → Peak competition; avoid auctions, focus proprietary sourcing, discipline
  on price
- **Small-cap earnings declining + wage inflation persistent + HY stable**
  → Portfolio company operating headwinds; focus on operational improvement,
  delay exits, protect covenants
