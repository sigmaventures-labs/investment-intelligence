[3-5 sentences. Why this business, why now, what's the value creation plan,

## Variant Perception / Edge
[Why can we win this deal at an attractive price and/or create value others

## Business Overview
[What the company does, revenue model, customer profile, competitive
position, end-market dynamics. Focus on aspects relevant to the value

## Value Creation Plan
[THE most important section. Detail each lever with specifics:

### Revenue Growth
[Organic initiatives (pricing, cross-sell, new products, geographic

### Margin Expansion
[Operational improvements: procurement savings, SG&A rationalization,

[Why will exit multiple exceed entry? Larger scale, improved quality of

[Leverage at entry, deleveraging path, dividend recapitalization potential,

For each lever: size of impact on returns, confidence level, execution risk.]

[Quality of existing team, retention plan, gaps to fill, incentive

## PE-Specific Key Variables

Typical high-sensitivity variables:
- **Entry multiple**: The price you pay is the single biggest determinant
  of returns
- **Revenue CAGR**: Organic + inorganic growth through hold period
- **EBITDA margin at exit**: Reflects operational value creation
- **Exit multiple**: Drives terminal value; justify vs. entry and market
- **Leverage**: Amplifies equity returns but adds risk
- **Hold period**: Longer holds compress IRR but may increase MOIC

## Returns Methodology Notes

- **LBO model**: The standard PE returns framework. Project EBITDA through
  hold period, apply exit multiple, subtract net debt, calculate equity
  return (IRR and MOIC).
- **Returns bridge**: Decompose gross IRR into components — revenue growth
  contribution, margin expansion, multiple expansion, leverage, and
  fee/transaction cost drag. This shows WHERE value is created.
- **Downside case**: Always model a case where the value creation plan
  partially fails. What's the floor return? Can you return capital?
- **Comparable transactions**: Entry multiple vs. recent deals in the
  sector. Precedent exits for multiple benchmarking.
