# Investment Memo — Public Equities (L/S)

## Memo Template

```markdown
# [COMPANY NAME] ([TICKER]) — [LONG/SHORT]

**Analyst**: [Name] | **Date**: [Date]
**Current Price**: $[X] | **Target Price**: $[Y] | **Upside/Downside**: [Z]%
**Market Cap**: $[X]B | **EV**: $[X]B
**Sector**: [Sector] | **Time Horizon**: [X months]

---

## Executive Summary
[3-5 sentences. Thesis, variant perception, target return, primary catalyst.
Compelling enough that a PM who reads nothing else understands the trade.]

## Variant Perception
[What consensus believes → why they're wrong → what you see differently.
Be specific: "Market models 8% volume growth but misses the Mexico capacity
expansion buried in the 10-K supplement" — not "undervalued."]

## Business Overview
[Revenue mix by segment/geography, business model, unit economics,
competitive moat, management quality. Thesis-relevant only.]

## Key Drivers
[3-5 variables. For each: what it is, consensus estimate, your estimate,
evidence. Numbered with substance.]

## Catalyst Path
[Time-bound observable events. For each: what, when, expected impact,
probability-weighted contribution.]

## Valuation
[Primary methodology (DCF/comps/SOTP) with key assumptions.
Cross-check with alternative method.
Sensitivity table on 2-3 most important assumptions.
Transparent target price derivation.]

## Risk Factors
[3-5 material risks. Each: risk, probability, impact, mitigant/monitor.]

## Position Sizing & Risk Management
[Size %, stop-loss, hedging, portfolio correlation.]

## Key Metrics to Monitor
[5-7 leading indicators, reporting frequency, reassessment thresholds.]
```

## Equity-Specific Key Variables

Typical high-sensitivity variables for equity memos:
- Revenue growth (volume × price, or organic vs. acquired)
- Operating/EBITDA margin trajectory
- Valuation multiple (P/E, EV/EBITDA — justify which is primary)
- Capital allocation (buybacks, M&A, capex, dividends)
- 1-2 idiosyncratic variables (regulatory outcome, market share, product cycle)

## Valuation Methodology Notes

- **DCF**: Use for companies with predictable cash flows. Always show WACC
  sensitivity and terminal value assumptions (terminal growth or exit multiple).
- **Comps**: Use EV-based multiples for capital structure neutrality. Justify
  premium/discount to peers.
- **Sum-of-parts**: Use when segments trade at different multiples or when a
  catalyst will unlock hidden value (spinoff, asset sale).
- Always cross-check primary method with at least one alternative.
