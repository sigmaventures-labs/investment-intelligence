# Investment Memo — Real Estate

## Memo Template

```markdown
# [PROPERTY NAME / ADDRESS] — [ACQUISITION / DISPOSITION / REFI / DEVELOPMENT]

**Analyst**: [Name] | **Date**: [Date]
**Property Type**: [Office/Multi/Industrial/Retail/Other] | **Submarket**: [Market]
**Acquisition Price**: $[X]M | **Price/SF (or /Unit)**: $[X]
**Going-In Cap Rate**: [X]% | **Stabilized Cap Rate**: [X]%
**Unlevered IRR**: [X]% | **Levered IRR**: [X]% | **Equity Multiple**: [X.Xx]
**Hold Period**: [X years] | **LTV**: [X]%

---

## Executive Summary
[3-5 sentences. Why this asset, why this basis, what's the business plan,
what's the return profile, and what's the edge vs. other bidders.]

## Variant Perception / Edge
[Why can we acquire this below intrinsic value or execute a business plan
others can't? Sources of edge: off-market sourcing, operational expertise,
local market knowledge, capital structure creativity, entitlement/zoning
relationships, tenant relationships.]

## Asset & Market Overview
[Physical description, condition, age, recent capex. Submarket fundamentals:
vacancy, rent growth, absorption, new supply pipeline, employment drivers.
Comparable transactions. Thesis-relevant only.]

## Business Plan / Value Creation
[The core of an RE memo. Detail the specific plan:
- Leasing strategy (new leases, renewals, mark-to-market opportunity)
- Capital improvements (scope, cost, expected rent premium)
- Operational improvements (expense reduction, management upgrade)
- Repositioning or change of use (if applicable)
- Development / entitlement (if applicable)
For each lever: current state, target state, cost, timeline, evidence it's achievable.]

## Tenancy & Lease Analysis
[Rent roll summary: occupancy, WALT, near-term expirations, tenant credit,
in-place rents vs. market, lease structure (NNN/gross/modified gross),
rent escalation provisions, renewal probability assessment.]

## Financing
[Proposed capital structure: senior debt terms (rate, term, amortization,
covenants, prepayment), mezzanine or preferred (if applicable), total equity
requirement, sources of equity. Debt service coverage ratio at acquisition
and at stabilization. Refinancing risk assessment.]

## Returns Analysis
[Unlevered and levered IRR, equity multiple, cash-on-cash by year.
Sensitivity table on 2-3 key assumptions (exit cap rate × rent growth,
or cap rate × vacancy, etc.). Comparison to fund hurdle rate.]

## Risk Factors
[3-5 material risks: tenant default/vacancy, market softening, capex
overruns, interest rate / refinancing risk, environmental/regulatory.
Each: risk, probability, impact, mitigant.]

## Exit Strategy
[Primary and secondary exit paths. Target exit cap rate with justification.
Buyer universe. Hold period optionality (extend if market is soft).]

## Key Metrics to Monitor
[Leasing velocity, submarket vacancy, rent comps, construction starts,
tenant financial health, interest rate trajectory, capex burn vs. budget.]
```

## RE-Specific Key Variables

Typical high-sensitivity variables:
- **Rent growth / mark-to-market**: Gap between in-place rents and market
- **Vacancy / absorption**: Current and projected occupancy
- **Exit cap rate**: The single most sensitive variable in most RE underwriting
- **Financing rate**: Acquisition and refinancing cost of debt
- **Capex / TI budget**: Cost to execute the business plan
- **NOI margin**: Operating expenses as % of revenue

## Valuation Methodology Notes

- **Direct capitalization**: NOI / cap rate. Use stabilized NOI, not trailing.
  Justify cap rate with recent comps.
- **Discounted cash flow**: 10-year hold with annual NOI projection and
  terminal value (exit cap rate on Year 11 NOI). Discount rate = unlevered
  required return.
- **Replacement cost**: What would it cost to build today? Meaningful floor
  for well-located assets.
- **Comparable transactions**: $/SF, $/unit, cap rate vs. recent trades.
  Adjust for age, quality, tenancy, market.
- Always present levered and unlevered returns separately.
