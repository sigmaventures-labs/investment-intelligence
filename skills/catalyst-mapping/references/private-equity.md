# Catalyst Mapping — Private Equity

## Catalyst Taxonomy

| Category | Examples | Lead Time |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Value Creation Milestones** | Operational initiatives go-live, pricing actions, new product launches, system integrations, management hires, facility consolidations | Plan-driven |
| **M&A / Bolt-on** | Bolt-on pipeline closure, LOI execution, integration milestones, synergy realization deadlines | Deal-specific |
| **Financial Events** | Quarterly/annual board reporting, covenant compliance tests, dividend recap assessment points, credit facility maturity | Scheduled |
| **Exit Preparation** | Vendor due diligence completion, management presentation prep, banker engagement, dual-track (IPO + sale) milestones | 6-18 months pre-exit |
| **Exit Execution** | Process launch, management presentations, final bid deadline, exclusivity, signing, regulatory approval, closing | 3-9 month process |
| **Fund Lifecycle** | Investment period end, harvest period milestones, GP clawback trigger points, fund extension votes, LP advisory committee meetings | Scheduled (per LPA) |
| **Market / Macro Windows** | M&A market conditions, IPO windows, credit market availability, sector multiple cycles, comparable transaction announcements | Monitored |
| **Portfolio Company External** | Customer contract renewals, regulatory approvals, competitive product launches, key hire/departure, litigation outcomes | Mixed |

## Asymmetry Assessment — PE-Specific

- **Exit window asymmetry**: Favorable M&A/IPO markets are episodic. Missing a
  window can add 1-2 years of hold time, compressing IRR significantly even if
  MOIC stays flat. The cost of delay is non-linear for IRR.
- **Bolt-on execution risk**: If the returns model depends on 3 bolt-ons at 6-8x
  and you've closed zero after 18 months, the plan is failing. Each missed
  acquisition is a negative catalyst for underwritten returns.
- **Covenant breach**: Unlike equities where drawdowns are mark-to-market, PE
  covenant breaches trigger real consequences (cash sweeps, equity cures,
  lender control). These are hard catalysts.
- **Fund lifecycle pressure**: As a fund approaches investment period end or
  extension deadlines, the GP's incentives shift. Late-fund deals may be done
  under time pressure. Portfolio companies in harvest period face increasing
  exit pressure.

## Source Hierarchy for Catalyst Discovery

1. 100-day plan and value creation roadmap (operational milestones)
2. Board materials and portfolio company management reporting
3. Credit agreement (covenants, maturity, amendment dates)
4. LPA and fund documents (investment period, extensions, key dates)
5. M&A market intelligence (banker activity, comparable processes)
6. Portfolio company contracts (customer renewals, supplier agreements)
7. Industry events and competitive intelligence

## Calendar Time Horizon

PE catalysts span the full hold period (typically 3-7 years). Organize by phase:

- **Year 1 (100-day plan)**: Dense with operational milestones. Monthly tracking.
- **Years 2-3 (value creation)**: Quarterly milestones around growth, margin,
  and bolt-on execution.
- **Years 3-5 (exit preparation)**: Exit readiness milestones, market window
  assessment, process timing.
- **Fund-level**: Overlay fund lifecycle events (period end, extensions).
