# Catalyst Mapping — Public Equities

## Catalyst Taxonomy

| Category | Examples | Lead Time |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Earnings & Guidance** | Quarterly earnings, pre-announcements, guidance revisions, analyst days | Scheduled |
| **Regulatory & Legal** | FDA decisions (PDUFA), antitrust rulings, patent expirations, settlements | Often scheduled |
| **Macro & Policy** | FOMC meetings, CPI/NFP releases, tariff decisions, elections | Scheduled |
| **Corporate Actions** | M&A, spinoffs, buybacks, management changes, capital raises, insider txns | Often unscheduled |
| **Industry / Competitive** | Competitor earnings (read-throughs), trade shows, product launches, contracts | Mixed |
| **Data & Channel Checks** | Monthly data (SSS, auto sales, web traffic), surveys, alternative data | Varies |
| **Technical / Flow** | Index rebalances, options expiry, lock-up expirations, short interest | Scheduled |

## Asymmetry Assessment Tools

- **Options-implied move**: ATM straddle pricing around the event gauges the
  market's expected magnitude
- **Consensus estimates**: Whisper numbers vs. published consensus — gap
  indicates potential surprise direction
- **Historical earnings reaction**: How has this stock moved on the last
  4-8 earnings prints? Is there a pattern of over/under-reaction?
- **Short interest**: Elevated SI creates asymmetric upside on positive surprises
  (short squeeze potential)

## Source Hierarchy for Catalyst Discovery

1. Company IR calendar and SEC filings (earnings dates, annual meetings)
2. Regulatory body calendars (FDA PDUFA, FCC, EPA)
3. Economic calendar (FOMC, BLS, Census)
4. Industry event calendars (CES, JPM Healthcare, etc.)
5. Sell-side event trackers and earnings calendars
6. News flow and M&A speculation
