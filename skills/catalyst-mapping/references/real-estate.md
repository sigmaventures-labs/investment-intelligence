# Catalyst Mapping — Real Estate

## Catalyst Taxonomy

| Category | Examples | Lead Time |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Lease Events** | Lease expirations, renewal options, rent escalations, free rent burn-off, co-tenancy clauses triggering | Scheduled (from rent roll) |
| **Capital Markets** | Debt maturity/refinancing, rate resets on floating debt, loan covenant tests, CMBS special servicing triggers | Scheduled |
| **Construction & Development** | Entitlement/zoning approvals, building permits, TCO/CO milestones, construction completion, phased delivery | Mix of scheduled and uncertain |
| **Regulatory & Tax** | Tax assessment appeals, rent control changes, zoning amendments, environmental remediation deadlines, Opportunity Zone deadlines | Often scheduled |
| **Market Data Releases** | Quarterly vacancy/absorption reports (CBRE, JLL, Cushman), construction starts data, CPI (rent escalation trigger), FOMC (cap rate impact) | Scheduled |
| **Transaction / Disposition** | Marketing launch, offer deadlines, PSA contingency expirations, closing conditions, 1031 exchange deadlines | Deal-specific |
| **Portfolio / Fund** | Fund investment period expiration, extension decisions, capital call schedules, distribution dates, NAV reporting | Scheduled |

## Asymmetry Assessment — RE-Specific

- **Lease-up risk asymmetry**: If the business plan depends on leasing 50K SF
  by Q4, the downside of missing (extended carry cost, covenant breach) is
  often much larger than the upside of beating (marginal additional NOI).
  This creates structural negative asymmetry around lease-up milestones.
- **Refinancing binary**: Debt maturity is a hard catalyst. Inability to
  refinance can force a sale or equity injection. Assess probability of
  refi at acceptable terms vs. need for recap.
- **Entitlement risk**: Zoning/permit approvals are often binary with long
  timelines. Positive resolution can unlock large value; denial can strand
  capital. Assess political environment and precedent.

## Source Hierarchy for Catalyst Discovery

1. Rent roll and lease abstracts (lease events, escalations, options)
2. Loan documents (maturity, covenants, extension options)
3. Development/construction schedule and permit status
4. Municipal calendar (zoning board meetings, tax appeal dates)
5. Brokerage research (market reports, comp surveys)
6. Economic calendar (FOMC, CPI, employment data)
7. Fund documents (PPM, side letters — investment period, extensions)

## Calendar Time Horizon

RE catalysts often operate on longer timescales than equities. Standard
calendar should extend 12-36 months. Critical to map:
- All lease expirations within 24 months (the near-term leasing challenge)
- Debt maturities within 36 months (refinancing runway)
- Entitlement/permit milestones (often 6-18 months)
