**Thesis Summary**

The rapid adoption of GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., Ozempic, Wegovy) for weight loss and diabetes management is reshaping consumer spending and healthcare costs in the US. This leads to reduced caloric intake and weight-related procedures, impacting food & beverage volumes, bariatric surgery demand, diabetes device usage, and managed care medical loss ratios (MLR). Beyond drug manufacturers, this theme affects a broad set of sectors including consumer staples, medical devices, managed care, and even adjacent industries through behavioral and cost-structure shifts.

---

**Causal Chain Diagram**

GLP-1 adoption → reduced appetite & weight loss →  
**1st order:** GLP-1 drug manufacturers (NVO, LLY) ↑; bariatric surgery & diabetes devices ↓; food & beverage volume ↓ →  
**2nd order:** snack food reformulation & volume pressure (MDLZ, PEP); gym/fitness pivot (LULU, NKE); managed care MLR improvement (UNH, HUM); medical device suppliers to bariatrics (GMED) ↓ →  
**3rd order:** lower chronic disease prevalence → life insurance risk improvement (LNC); reduced hospital admissions (HCA, CYH); airline fuel savings from lighter passengers (DAL, UAL); food packaging volume decline (WRK) ↓

**Pressure Test Notes**

- **Priced in?**  
  - NVO and LLY have seen strong stock appreciation; GLP-1 adoption is well-known and largely priced in.  
  - Bariatric surgery device impact (ISRG) is partially priced but less consensus on magnitude.  
  - Managed care benefits (UNH, HUM) are underappreciated due to long-dated nature of chronic disease cost savings.  
  - Snack food volume headwinds are emerging but offset by reformulation and premiumization; mixed market reaction.  
  - Third-order effects (life insurance, airlines) are niche and not priced in.

- **Counter-thesis:**  
  - GLP-1 adoption may plateau due to side effects, cost, or insurance coverage limits.  
  - Food & beverage volume declines may be offset by increased spending on premium or alternative products.  
  - Bariatric surgery may remain stable for patients who do not respond to drugs.  
  - Managed care savings may be offset by other rising healthcare costs.

- **Exposure purity:**  
  - Drug manufacturers have high thematic revenue exposure.  
  - Consumer staples and managed care have diluted exposure; theme is one of many factors.  
  - Third-order plays have low direct exposure but interesting optionality.

- **Investability:**  
  - All large- and mid-cap US equities with good liquidity.  
  - Some smaller names (WW, Medifast) are less liquid but offer pure short exposure.

2. **Intuitive Surgical (ISRG)**  
   Bariatric surgery displacement risk is high conviction and could pressure a meaningful portion of revenue. Market may be underestimating speed of GLP-1 adoption impact.

3. **Medifast (MED)**

4. **Lululemon Athletica (LULU)**  
   Fitness apparel demand may shift as consumers pivot from weight loss to fitness, creating a nuanced beneficiary beyond obvious health care names.

5. **Lincoln National (LNC)**  
   Life insurance mortality improvements from healthier populations is a subtle, long-term positive that is not priced in and rarely discussed.
